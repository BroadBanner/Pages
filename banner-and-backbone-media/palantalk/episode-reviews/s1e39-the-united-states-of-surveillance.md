### Terms and Concepts

- **Managed Narrative** — Shane's frame for the entire Iran conflict: not a war but a controlled information environment in which CENTCOM, Reuters, and the IRGC are all unreliable narrators.
- **AIS Spoofing** — Masking of vessel-tracking signals; detected from Iran on May 4th at 1500 UTC, indicating coordinated electronic warfare alongside the kinetic strikes on the USS Truxton and Mason.
- **Project Freedom** — The U.S. military escort operation for commercial vessels through the Strait of Hormuz, announced May 3rd, kicked off May 4th; in Shane's read, the catalyst event that killed the ceasefire.
- **Khalij Fars / Qader** — Iranian anti-ship and cruise missile families used May 7th against three U.S. destroyers transiting out of Hormuz; the first confirmed use of the anti-ship class Shane has been tracking in reserve.
- **CLOUD Act** — U.S. law cited by Switzerland as the reason Palantir data risk "cannot be technically prevented" inside Swiss systems; the basis for Switzerland's contract termination after nine rejections over seven years.
- **Reconstitution Window** — Shane's read on the "ceasefire" period: time Iran used to rebuild force structure (to ~70% per ISW / CSIS / Critical Threats Project) and run the Pakistan overland resupply corridor, not time the U.S. used for diplomacy.
- **Belt and Road / Pakistan Corridor** — The overland resupply route Iran is using under the cover of Pakistan's role as peace intermediary; striking the corridor means striking the negotiator.
- **Petro-Yuan** — The Chinese-currency oil pricing arrangement Shane treats as the successor to the petrodollar; the UAE–China pivot during this conflict is his leading indicator.
- **THAAD Radar** — U.S. missile-detection system; UAE hosts two of eight U.S. batteries, with multiple destroyed in the conflict's first phase — a $5B capability loss that shortens detection windows on incoming Iranian missiles.
- **Bab al-Mandeb** — Houthi-controlled chokepoint at the southern Red Sea; not yet closed but at the highest threat posture since October 2025, with the USS Gerald R. Ford redeployed to the area after its onboard fire.
